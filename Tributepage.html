About Achivement Awards

Mahendra Singh Dhoni, commonly known as MS Dhoni, is a former Indian cricketer and one of the most successful captains in the history of Indian cricket.

Some key highlights of MS Dhoni's career include:

  • Successful captaincy in winning the ICC World Twenty20 (2007), ICC Cricket World Cup (2011), and ICC Champions Trophy (2013).
  • Recognition as one of the best finishers in limited-overs cricket.
  • Notable contributions as a wicketkeeper-batsman.

After his retirement from international cricket in 2020, Dhoni continued to play in domestic T20 leagues like the Indian Premier League (IPL).

His contributions to Indian cricket, both as a player and captain, have left an indelible mark on the sport.

Achievement

  • Captaincy Achievements:
    • Won ICC World Twenty20 in 2007.
    • Won ICC Cricket World Cup in 2011.
    • Won ICC Champions Trophy in 2013.
  • Test Cricket:
    • Led India to the No. 1 ranking in Test cricket in 2009.
  • One Day Internationals (ODIs):
    • Fastest century by an Indian wicketkeeper (off 72 balls against Pakistan in 2012).
    • Most sixes as captain in ODIs.
    • Most wins by an Indian captain in ODIs.
  • Indian Premier League (IPL):
    • Captain of Chennai Super Kings (CSK) - multiple IPL titles.
  • Other Achievements:
    • Received the Rajiv Gandhi Khel Ratna award in 2007.
    • Padma Shri award in 2009.

Awards

  • Rajiv Gandhi Khel Ratna (2007):

    India's highest honor given for achievements in sports. Dhoni received this award in 2007.

  • Padma Shri (2009):

    The fourth-highest civilian award in the Republic of India. Dhoni was honored with the Padma Shri in 2009.

  • ICC ODI Player of the Year (2008, 2009):

    Dhoni was named the ICC ODI Player of the Year in both 2008 and 2009 for his outstanding performances in One Day Internationals.

  • ICC World ODI XI (Multiple Years):

    Dhoni has been named in the ICC World ODI XI multiple times in recognition of his performances in One Day Internationals.

  • Padma Bhushan (2018):

    The third-highest civilian award in the Republic of India. Dhoni received the Padma Bhushan in 2018.

  • Outstanding Achievement in Sport (2012):

    Received this award at the Sahara India Sports Awards in 2012.
